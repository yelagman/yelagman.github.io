VR Self-Motion and Heading Perception Study

This project investigates how humans perceive their direction of self-motion (heading) in a virtual reality (VR) environment. The study reconciles conflicting findings on human heading perception errors by comparing three judgment methods: aligning a probe (position-based), pointing with a VR controller (orientation-based), and taking a step in the perceived direction. The experiment was conducted in a realistic outdoor VR environment built using Unity, and pilot data was collected to analyze the artifacts introduced by each judgment method.

Technologies used: Unity, VR Development, Data Analysis.
